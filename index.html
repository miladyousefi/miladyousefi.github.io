About Me

I am a computational researcher passionate about understanding the complexity of biological and healthcare systems through mathematics, data science, and artificial intelligence. My work sits at the intersection of biology, computer science, and applied mathematics, where I aim to turn challenging biological questions into structured computational problems. I enjoy transforming messy real-world biomedical data into meaningful insights, building predictive models, and developing intelligent tools that can support clinicians and scientists. Whether analyzing genetic patterns, modeling neural systems, or designing machine learning pipelines for medical diagnostics, I am driven by the goal of making complex systems easier to understand and clinically useful. Curiosity and persistence guide my research. I value teamwork and believe that ambitious scientific goals are best achieved through open collaboration, clear communication, and shared creativity. My experience working with interdisciplinary groups has strengthened my ability to translate between technical and biological domains, contribute to team problem-solving, and support an inclusive research culture. I am always excited to explore new ideas, learn from others, and contribute to projects that have meaningful impact on human health.
